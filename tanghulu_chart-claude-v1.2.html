糖胡芦图Tanghulu Chart

A candlestick tells you where price traveled. It says nothing about where the market actually traded. The Tanghulu Chart threads a day's volume-at-price distribution onto its high–low range like fruit on a skewer — so the shape of the candy, not just the stick, carries the story.

传统K线只能表达价格“到过哪里”，却无法表达市场“真正在哪里成交”。糖胡芦图把每日的价格—成交量分布， 串在当日最高价到最低价的“签子”上，像糖葫芦一样——让果子的大小，而不只是签子的长短，讲出这一天的故事。

核心问题The Core Problem

Two trading sessions can share identical open, high, low, and close values while representing completely different market structures — one where the close absorbed all the volume, another where buyers and sellers fought to a stalemate at opposite ends of the range. A candlestick cannot tell them apart. It draws the same four numbers, the same rectangle, the same two wicks — every time.

两根K线的开高低收可以完全相同，却对应着截然不同的成交结构——一根可能收盘前放量成交， 另一根可能是多空双方在区间两端反复拉锯、谁都没能真正占据主导。传统K线无法区分它们：只要OHLC相同， 画出来的矩形和上下影线就完全一样。

下面两个交易日的开高低收完全相同。画成K线，它们像素级一致；串成糖胡芦， 两者一眼可辨。

设计说明Design Explanation

The skewer 签子

A thin stick runs from the session low to the session high, poking out slightly at both ends — exactly the role a candlestick's wick plays today, just rendered as the physical object it's named after.

一根细签子从当日最低价贯穿到最高价，两端略微探出——它扮演的角色和传统K线的 上下影线完全一样，只是被画成了它名字里真正的那根签子。

The beads 果子

The price range is split into nine bins. Each bin becomes a bead, or a small round candy, centered at that price level. Bead area — not radius — scales with the volume traded at that price, so the eye reads volume the way it actually perceives area: two beads with twice the trading volume look roughly twice as "heavy," not four times as wide.

把当日价格区间切成9个价位段，每个价位段对应一颗“果子”，居中挂在对应价格上。 果子的面积（而不是半径）与该价位的成交量成正比——这样人眼感知到的“分量”才和真实成交量成正比， 成交量翻倍的两颗果子看起来是大约两倍“重”，而不是四倍宽。

Gloss vs. frost — the Value Area 价值区：亮糖衣与糖霜

Beads inside the session's Value Area (the contiguous price band holding ~70% of the day's volume, expanded outward from the POC) are rendered as glossy, saturated candy. Beads outside it — the thin tails of the distribution — are rendered pale and translucent, like plain fruit with barely a dusting of sugar. The eye is drawn to the glossy core without needing a single number.

落在价值区（从POC向两侧扩展、累计约70%成交量的连续价格带）内的果子，画成饱和有光泽的 糖衣质感；落在价值区之外的“尾部”果子，则画成浅淡、半透明的样子，像是没怎么裹糖的果肉。不需要任何数字标注， 视线自然会被有光泽的核心区吸引。

The gold ring — POC 金圈：POC

The single largest bead — the Point of Control — gets a thin gold ring. It is, quite literally, the shiniest piece of candy on the skewer, because it's the price the market agreed on most.

成交量最大的那颗果子——POC（成交量最大价位）——会被画上一圈细细的金边。它就是 整根糖葫芦上最亮的一颗，因为它是市场当天成交最多、认同度最高的价格。

Open and close — the ticks 开收盘：左右两撇

A short cream-colored tick reaches left from the skewer at the open, and right at the close — the same left/right convention traders already know from OHLC bar charts, borrowed intentionally so nothing new has to be learned for this part.

在开盘价位置向左伸出一小段浅色刻度，在收盘价位置向右伸出一小段——沿用了OHLC 柱状图里交易者早已熟悉的“左开右收”惯例，这部分刻意不引入任何新学习成本。

Red or green — the whole skewer 红绿：整根签子的颜色

The entire skewer's palette — beads, gold ring, ticks — shifts between hawthorn red (close ≥ open) and jade green (close < open), matching the red-up/green-down convention already standard across most of Asia's markets.

整根签子的配色（果子、金圈、开收刻度）会在“红色山楂糖葫芦”（收盘≥开盘）与 “绿色果脯糖葫芦”（收盘<开盘）之间切换，沿用了多数亚洲市场“红涨绿跌”的既有习惯。

读图指南Interpretation Guide

Read a single skewer top to bottom, then read the row left to right.

  1. Stick length = the day's full range, high to low.
    签子长度 = 当日最高价到最低价的完整波动区间。
  2. Biggest, gold-ringed bead = the POC — the price the market traded most.
    最大且带金圈的果子 = POC，当日成交最集中的价格。
  3. Glossy cluster = the Value Area — where ~70% of the day's business actually happened.
    有光泽的果子聚集区 = 价值区，约70%成交量真正发生的地方。
  4. Left tick / right tick = open / close; color = up (red) or down (green) day.
    左刻度/右刻度 = 开盘/收盘；颜色 = 上涨（红）或下跌（绿）。
  5. Scan bead sizes across days to see volume migrate to new price zones over time.
    横向比较多日果子大小，观察成交重心随时间的迁移。
  6. Turn on POC Trail to see whether "fair value" is climbing, falling, or holding — independent of what the close price is doing.
    打开“POC连线”，观察“公允价值”是在上移、下移还是原地不动—— 这和收盘价的走势可能并不一致。
  7. Turn on Value-Area Ribbon to see whether the market's accepted range is widening (a possible breakout building) or narrowing (consolidation).
    打开“价值区带”，观察市场认可的价格区间是在扩张（可能酝酿突破） 还是在收窄（进入盘整）。

The Tanghulu Chart is not a replacement for the candlestick — it asks a different question. A candlestick answers "where did price go?" in one glance across hundreds of symbols. A Tanghulu Chart answers "who was actually in control, and at what price?" for the handful of symbols worth that closer look.

糖胡芦图不是要取代K线，而是回答一个不同的问题。K线用一眼扫过成百上千个标的， 回答“价格去了哪里”；糖胡芦图则为少数值得深入研究的标的，回答“谁真正掌控了成交，以及在什么价格上”。

后续扩展方向Future Extensions

  1. Real tick data. Replace the simulated Gaussian-mixture volume profile with actual exchange tick prints binned by price — the bin-building function is already isolated so this is a data-layer swap, not a rendering rewrite.
    接入真实逐笔成交数据，用真实成交价格分布取代模拟的高斯混合分布—— 由于分箱逻辑已独立封装，这只是数据层的替换，不需要重写渲染逻辑。
  2. Day-over-day delta markers. A small arrow or shift-indicator showing which direction the POC or Value Area moved versus the prior session, making migration legible without staring at the whole trail.
    增加逐日POC/价值区迁移的箭头或位移标记，无需盯着整条连线也能 快速看出迁移方向。
  3. Order-flow overlays. Encode bid/ask imbalance or open interest as bead outline thickness or a second, inner ring, layering order-flow context onto the existing volume-at-price shape.
    用果子外圈粗细或内圈的方式叠加买卖盘失衡或持仓量信息， 在现有的量价形状上再叠加一层盘口信息。
  4. Volatility-adaptive binning. Size the number of beads per skewer to the session's ATR rather than a fixed count, so quiet days aren't over-segmented and violent days aren't under-resolved.
    按当日ATR自适应调整果子（分箱）数量，而不是固定数量， 避免平静日过度切分、剧烈波动日分辨率不足。
  5. Live, growing beads. For intraday use, beads could grow in real time as volume accrues at each price, turning the chart into a live auction-in-progress view rather than a end-of-day summary.
    用于盘中场景时，果子可随成交量实时增长， 让图表呈现“正在进行的竞价过程”，而不只是收盘后的静态总结。
  6. Weekly / monthly composite skewers. Merge several daily bead sets into one higher-timeframe skewer to study structural shifts over swings rather than single sessions.
    将多日的果子分布合并为周线/月线级别的复合签子， 用于研究更大级别摆动中的结构变化，而不仅是单日结构。
  7. Shareable single-day exports. Let a single skewer be exported as an annotated image for research notes or trade journals, the way a single candle is screenshotted today.
    支持将单根糖胡芦导出为带标注的图片， 用于研究笔记或交易日志，就像今天截取单根K线一样方便。
